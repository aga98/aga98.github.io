1. NIAGARA FALLS

Niagara Falls is the collective name for three waterfalls that straddle the international border between the Canadian province of Ontario and the American state of New York. They form the southern end of the Niagara Gorge.From largest to smallest, the three waterfalls are the Horseshoe Falls, the American Falls and the Bridal Veil Falls. The Horseshoe Falls lies on the border of the United States and Canada[1] with the American Falls entirely on the United States' side, separated by Goat Island. The smaller Bridal Veil Falls are also on the United States' side, separated from the American Falls by Luna Island.Located on the Niagara River, which drains Lake Erie into Lake Ontario, the combined falls form the highest flow rate of any waterfall in North America that has a vertical drop of more than 165 feet (50 m).

2. NIAGARA ON THE LAKE

Niagara-on-the-Lake is a Canadian city located in southern Ontario, where the Niagara River reaches Lake Ontario. It is known as the most beautiful city in Ontario and has a long and distinguished history. It is a tourist destination that many people visit all year round for its varied culinary offer, its wineries, for the Shaw Festival, for its hiking trails and for the rich local agriculture. The streets and buildings are very careful and have a very romantic touch. You will find charming accommodations, from 5-star hotels to cozy B&Bs.
